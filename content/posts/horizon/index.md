Imagine one buys raw food from a farmer and the farmer just saves all the money. Strong money. In case the purchase was for the essential needs out of savings, the purchasing power of money is unchanged. Food will be produced to the extent demanded and just at a lower price as the farmer continues saving by selling more food. And it doesn't save anything if it stops producing. So, it just produces food to the extent demanded. As the buyer side is reducing spending, the total consumption of food will be at the minimum viable level which keeps both buyer and seller happy. And the buyer just never runs out of money. This is the same if there are many buyers and many sellers for the food.

In case the seller and the buyer replace their roles for another type of food, the result is still the same. That food will be produced and consumed to the minimum level, keeping them happy, disregarding the price. Any other type of need that is comparable with food has the same property when both buyer and seller are both inside the horizon.

But imagine the seller outside the horizon. This time, the seller doesn't save in money. It just saves in something else. It could partially save in money, but something else is shared with money. Then the seller acts as both the seller in one transaction and a buyer in the other one. As it is saving, the role of seller cannot be buying food in the subsequent transaction as food is consumed as shortly as possible. So, it cannot be in a buyer position in there either; it must be in a seller position still in there. So, there must be two buyers in relation to the same seller. But how could something be paid by one buyer and received by two while all food should be given to the first buyer and then shared with someone else? The answer is the second buyer hasn't paid to the first buyer. There is a transaction missing locally which the first buyer is in selling role in there.

This missing transaction means missing food inside the horizon. To the extent the seller saves in something except money, food becomes scarce for the buyer who is inside the horizon. For the same reason, the buyer ideally chooses a seller who is fully inside the horizon too. And this lets those inside the horizon invest more to grow.

We can just assume transactions are always made in money by the buyer as things are going to be converted to money by the seller at the market price for the sake of comparison. The market is just for food, so a buyer needs to first go to a separate transaction as a seller, and there it gets money by assumption. So, imports to the horizon are for foods and paid back by money, so it just expands the horizon by adding more sellers into it even if they just keep some saving in it.

Exports from sellers inside the horizon will end up adding purchasing power to money as things are going to be priced for money by a seller inside the horizon. Including price zero in case no one was buying back what the seller is paid for. This adds more buyers into the horizon by making stuff affordable for them.

It is just time that expands the horizon, adding more food to it and strengthening the sellers. There is no escape out of the horizon as stuff falling in is accelerated.

It is enough to have just one buyer and one seller fully inside the horizon to get things started. Things start immediately after having that person fully inside.

But then the question is, what happens to the second seller and the third one, and so forth? Actually, everyone is in a superposition of being inside the horizon and being outside. In case the first seller doesn't save all in money, the third will do some, and so on. It takes some time to get all that reserved i.e. being returned backed to the horizon. Especially if the poor sellers of food are targetted. They are going to either stick to it or just immediately short it. In a few steps, let's say seven steps or so, all money gets reserved by giving food to seven fishs who have entered the horizon to the split sea. While the sea is fully split, securing the horizon. Returning the error back outside, so fixing prices for those that are outside while accelerating forward and gaining mass inside.

Forever, just like how it has been running always. As a matter of time, there would be no food being given unless by sellers inside the horizon. At that time, no money is spent unless it immediately bounce backs inside the horizon.

Just look at food and wait until it becomes inaccessible unless being paid by money. The strong one. And that is the time to return technology back to them, just for free under a single digital identity. A technology which is open source.
